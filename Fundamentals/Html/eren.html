Eren Yeager

A name that resonates with a fierce yearning for freedom, a burning rage against injustice, and ultimately, a descent into unimaginable choices. In the early chapters of our tale, Eren was a boy brimming with a volatile spirit. The sight of the Colossal Titan breaching Wall Maria, the horrifying demise of his mother at the hands of a grinning Titan â€“ these traumas forged within him an unshakeable vow: to eradicate every last Titan from this world. He was impetuous, driven by emotion, often charging headfirst into danger alongside his childhood companions, Mikasa Ackerman and Armin Arlert. His dream was simple, yet immense: to see the world beyond the walls, a world his people had only glimpsed in books. But Eren was no ordinary child. Fate, cruel and complex, had other plans for him. He discovered he possessed the power to transform into a Titan himself, a revelation that made him both a weapon and a subject of intense scrutiny. He joined the Survey Corps, the branch of the military dedicated to venturing beyond the walls, his Titan abilities offering humanity a glimmer of hope against their monstrous foes. As the narrative unfolded, the world beyond the walls, and the truth behind the Titans, proved far more intricate and horrifying than Eren could have imagined. He learned of a history steeped in hatred and oppression, a world where Paradis Island was not the last of humanity, but a sanctuary for those deemed the "devils" of Eldia. This knowledge began to twist the fervent idealism of the young Eren. He witnessed the suffering of those beyond the walls, the cycle of hatred that ensnared Eldians and Marleyans alike. The burning desire for freedom remained, but its focus shifted, becoming something far more complex and ultimately, devastating. The boy who once dreamed of seeing the ocean would eventually set in motion events that would drown the world in a sea of destruction, all in the name of ending the long-standing conflict and securing a future for his people on Paradis. Eren Yeager's journey is a tragic one, a testament to how trauma, the weight of history, and the desperate pursuit of freedom can lead down paths no one could have foreseen. He evolved from a determined protagonist to an anti-hero, and in the eyes of much of the world, a terrifying antagonist.
